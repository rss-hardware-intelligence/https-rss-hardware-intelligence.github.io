1st Workshop on Robot Hardware-Aware Intelligence

RSS 2025, Los Angeles, California | Wednesday, June 25th, 2025

rss.hardware.intelligence@gmail.com

Overview

Advances in artificial intelligence and robotics have increasingly relied on better algorithms or larger datasets while often overlooking the critical role of hardware. In this workshop, we aim to addresses the wide gap between mechanical engineers, who design complex and innovative hardware, and computer scientists, who develop intelligent software for simplified or standard platforms. Bridging this divide is essential for creating integrated, intelligent systems that fully exploit the potential of both fields. We will will also explore emerging topics such as language models and foundation models for design, focusing on scalable, data-driven methods for generative and hardware optimization tasks. By bringing together researchers from robotics, machine learning, and mechanical engineering, in this workshop we aims to foster interdisciplinary collaboration, present cutting-edge research, and propose new methodologies for unifying hardware and intelligence in robotics.

Areas of Interest

We invite contributions and discussions on topics related to hardware-aware intelligence, including:

  • Hardware Design for Learning: Exploring how compliant mechanisms and novel dexterous hand designs facilitate learning-based control.
  • Low-Cost Robotics: Prototyping intelligent systems using accessible platforms and tools.
  • Sensor Optimization: Placement, design, and integration of sensor suites to improve perception and learning policies.
  • Generative and Evolutionary Hardware Design: Leveraging generative methods and evolutionary algorithms for optimizing robot morphology and function.
  • Co-Design of Control and Hardware: Joint optimization of physical design and control policies for high-performance robotic systems.
  • Manufacture-in-the-Loop Design: Iterative design approaches integrating fabrication processes.
  • Learning for Unconventional Hardware Platforms: Developing modeling approaches tailored to exploit unique properties of unconventional hardware.
